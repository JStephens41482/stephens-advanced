Page 3 of 4 · The Money

Five streams in. Three pots out.

Money flows where it naturally lives — Riker subs to the software side, marketplace cuts to the network side, in-house jobs + ext sales + training to the joint side. When one side helps the other, we trade equity at fair value.

The recursive flywheel — Riker isn't just back-office automation. Riker pushes hot jobs from the network to its subscribers before anyone else sees them. More contractors on the free network → more jobs on the marketplace board → more hot-job alerts → more reason to pay $800/mo for Riker → more revenue → better Riker → better alerts → more contractors. Riker pays for itself in new revenue, not just labor savings.
The equity swap mechanism — when you bring contractors into your network and they end up paying Jon's Software Co for Riker, that's value Bobby created. When Jon's Riker triggers a swap and Bobby's Network Co captures 15%, that's value Jon created. We trade equity stakes between the two companies at fair value to keep it honest.
